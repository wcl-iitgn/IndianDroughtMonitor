Dryness Categories

The India Drought Monitor uses a five-category system to identify the unusualness of dry conditions. The categories range from D0 (Abnormally Dry) through D4 (Exceptional Drought). Each weekly map represents an integrated assessment derived from objective hydro-meteorological indicators (precipitation, soil moisture, runoff and related variables).

D0 Abnormally Dry
D1 Moderate Drought
D2 Severe Drought
D3 Extreme Drought
D4 Exceptional Drought

The India Drought Monitor is developed by the Water and Climate Lab at the Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar, drawing on hydro-meteorological data from the India Meteorological Department and the Central Water Commission, satellite products from ISRO/NRSC, and global datasets from NASA.
