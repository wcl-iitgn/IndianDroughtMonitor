# India Drought Monitor — National Summary for week ending 2026-05-20

The national drought situation this week shows a majority of the area remaining in the Normal class, with a small portion experiencing drought conditions. The total area in the D0+ drought class was 15.3% this week, which represents a contraction of 0.9 percentage points compared to the previous week. The cumulative distribution across the drought classes is as follows: Normal at 84.7%, D0+ at 15.3%, D1+ at 3.1%, D2+ at 0.2%, D3+ at 0.0%, and D4 at 0.0%. The states most affected by drought were NCT of Delhi with 52.2% in drought, followed by Manipur at 42.3%, Mizoram at 40.8%, Arunanchal Pradesh at 40.3%, Jammu & Kashmir at 38.9%, and Meghalaya at 34.1%. Conversely, West Bengal reported 0.0% in drought, and Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Telangana reported 1.0%, 2.1%, and 3.4% in drought, respectively.
