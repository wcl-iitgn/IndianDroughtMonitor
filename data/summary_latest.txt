# India Drought Monitor — National Summary for week ending 2026-05-20

The national drought conditions improved slightly this week, with the cumulative area under drought conditions contracting to 15.3% of India's total land area. This represents a decrease of 0.9 percentage points compared to the previous week, following a further reduction of 1.0 percentage points from one month ago when the affected area stood at 17.2%. While the majority of the country remains in normal conditions at 84.7%, the remaining 15.3% of the nation is experiencing at least abnormally dry conditions, with a small fraction classified as moderate or severe. The contraction in drought area is primarily driven by recovery in key regions, though specific state-level impacts remain significant. The National Capital Territory of Delhi continues to face the most severe localized impact, with 52.2% of its area under drought, followed by Manipur at 42.3% and Mizoram at 40.8%. Other states heavily affected include Arunachal Pradesh, Jammu & Kashmir, and Meghalaya. Conversely, the southern and western states show notably better conditions, with West Bengal reporting zero area under drought and Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Telangana recording minimal impact at 1.0%, 2.1%, and 3.4% respectively.
