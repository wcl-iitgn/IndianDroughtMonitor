# India Drought Monitor — National Summary for week ending 2026-05-20

As of the week ending May 20, 2026, the national area experiencing drought conditions has contracted to 15.3 percent, marking a slight improvement from the previous week. This reduction of 0.9 percentage points brings the cumulative area in D0 or worse conditions down from 16.2 percent last week to 15.3 percent, while the area classified as D1 or worse has decreased to 3.1 percent. The most severe categories, D2 and above, remain minimal, accounting for only 0.2 percent and 0.0 percent of the total area, respectively.

Geographically, the NCT of Delhi remains the most impacted region, with 52.2 percent of its area under drought conditions, followed closely by Manipur at 42.3 percent, Mizoram at 40.8 percent, and Arunanchal Pradesh at 40.3 percent. Jammu & Kashmir and Meghalaya also face significant challenges, with 38.9 percent and 34.1 percent of their respective areas affected. In contrast, the western and eastern states show notably better conditions; West Bengal reports zero percent drought coverage, while Rajasthan, Gujarat, and Telangana are affected in only 1.0, 2.1, and 3.4 percent of their areas, respectively.
