# India Drought Monitor — National Summary for week ending 2026-03-25

For the week ending March 25, 2026, about 19.5% of India's area was in some level of drought (D0 or worse), with 80.5% at normal conditions. Severe-to-extreme drought (D2 or worse) covered roughly 0.9% of the country. Compared with the previous week, the drought-affected area contracted (-8.1 percentage points). Conditions were driven by the balance of pre-monsoon rainfall, soil moisture and runoff captured in the Combined Drought Index, with the most persistent stress across rain-fed parts of peninsular and central India.

Regional conditions this week:
- North: Widespread drought. About 57% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- Northwest: Largely normal conditions. About 3% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
- Northeast: Moderate, scattered drought. About 22% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
- East: Moderate, scattered drought. About 28% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- Central: Moderate, scattered drought. About 21% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- West: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 8% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
- South: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 6% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
