# India Drought Monitor — National Summary for week ending 2026-06-10

The national drought situation this week shows a majority of the area in a Normal condition, with 81.5% of India falling into the Normal class. The total area experiencing drought conditions, specifically D0 and above, was 18.5% this week. This figure reflects a contraction in the drought area compared to the previous week, as the total drought area was 19.6% last week and 16.2% one month ago, indicating a decrease of 1.1 percentage points week-on-week. The most affected regions by drought were the NCT of Delhi, with 65.2% in drought, followed by Manipur at 63.0% and Arunanchal Pradesh at 51.6%. Maharashtra and Jammu & Kashmir were also significantly affected, each accounting for 41.9% of the drought area. Conversely, Sikkim had 0.0% in drought, and Rajasthan, West Bengal, and Nagaland were the least affected states, with drought percentages of 0.0%, 0.3%, 0.5%, and 0.7% respectively.
