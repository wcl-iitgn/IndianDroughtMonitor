# India Drought Monitor — National Summary for week ending 2026-06-03

The national drought situation this week shows a distribution across the drought classes, with 80.4% of the area in the Normal category and 19.6% in the D0+ category. The total area affected by drought, specifically D0+, was 19.6% this week, which represents a contraction of 0.5 percentage points compared to the previous week's 20.1%. Looking further back, the area in D0+ was 15.0% approximately one month ago.

Gujarat exhibited the highest level of drought impact, with 85.2% of its area in drought. Other states with significant drought presence include the NCT of Delhi, which had 69.6% in drought, Arunanchal Pradesh at 52.8%, Jammu & Kashmir at 50.4%, Mizoram at 49.8%, and Meghalaya at 43.5%. Conversely, Sikkim reported 0.0% in drought, and Nagaland reported 0.7% in drought.

Regional conditions this week:
- North: Moderate, scattered drought. About 30% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- Northwest: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 5% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
- Northeast: Moderate, scattered drought. About 37% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). A notable share is in severe-or-worse categories (D2–D4).
- East: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 11% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- Central: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 11% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- West: Widespread drought. About 42% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- South: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 8% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
