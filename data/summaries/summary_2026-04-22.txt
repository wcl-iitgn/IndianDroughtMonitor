# India Drought Monitor — National Summary for week ending 2026-04-22

For the week ending April 22, 2026, about 17.2% of India's area was in some level of drought (D0 or worse), with 82.8% at normal conditions. Severe-to-extreme drought (D2 or worse) covered roughly 0.6% of the country. Compared with the previous week, the drought-affected area expanded (+3.7 percentage points). Conditions were driven by the balance of pre-monsoon rainfall, soil moisture and runoff captured in the Combined Drought Index, with the most persistent stress across rain-fed parts of peninsular and central India.

Regional conditions this week:
- North: Moderate, scattered drought. About 39% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- Northwest: Largely normal conditions. About 1% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
- Northeast: Moderate, scattered drought. About 25% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
- East: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 15% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- Central: Moderate, scattered drought. About 21% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- West: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 9% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
- South: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 10% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
