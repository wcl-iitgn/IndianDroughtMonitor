# India Drought Monitor — National Summary for week ending 2026-04-15

For the week ending April 15, 2026, about 13.6% of India's area was in some level of drought (D0 or worse), with 86.4% at normal conditions. Severe-to-extreme drought (D2 or worse) covered roughly 0.6% of the country. Compared with the previous week, the drought-affected area contracted (-0.6 percentage points). Conditions were driven by the balance of pre-monsoon rainfall, soil moisture and runoff captured in the Combined Drought Index, with the most persistent stress across rain-fed parts of peninsular and central India.

Regional conditions this week:

- South shows mostly normal conditions with localised dryness: about 6% of the region is in drought (D0–D4).
