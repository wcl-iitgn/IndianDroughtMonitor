{\large\bfseries\color{titleblue}Summary}\\[3pt]
{\justifying\normalsize\color{textblack}Most of India is currently under normal conditions, with drought impacts confined to scattered clusters rather than affecting large contiguous regions. Abnormally dry to moderate drought conditions (D0–D1) are observed across parts of northwestern India, central India, and the Northeast, while severe to extreme drought conditions (D2–D3) occur in localized hotspots in Maharashtra, Haryana–Delhi, Jammu \& Kashmir, and several northeastern states. \par\medskip The most drought-affected states include Maharashtra, where about 42\% of the area falls under drought categories (D0–D4), followed by Haryana, Arunachal Pradesh, Jammu \& Kashmir, Meghalaya, and Mizoram. Exceptional drought (D4) is limited to small areas in Maharashtra, Manipur, Mizoram, and Jammu \& Kashmir.\par}
