# India Drought Monitor — National Summary for week ending 2026-05-20

As the pre-monsoon period draws to a close, drought continues to affect a modest share of the country, with about 15.3% of India's area in some level of drought (D0 or worse) and the remaining 84.7% at normal conditions. Severe-to-extreme drought (D2 or worse) remains spatially limited at the national scale, though it is locally intense across much of peninsular and northeastern India.

Conditions eased slightly over the past week: the drought-affected area contracted by roughly 0.9 percentage points compared with the previous week, continuing a gradual decline from about 17.2% a month ago.

The most affected states this week are concentrated in the south and northeast, where Andhra Pradesh, Assam, Chhattisgarh, Goa, Gujarat and Haryana each show nearly their entire area in some drought category, with large shares in severe-to-extreme classes. Conditions are most favourable over the eastern Gangetic plains and the northeast Himalaya, with West Bengal, Arunachal Pradesh, Rajasthan and Uttar Pradesh among the least affected. A normal-to-above-normal monsoon onset would be needed to materially ease the remaining deficits.
