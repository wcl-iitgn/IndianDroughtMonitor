# India Drought Monitor — National Summary for week ending 2026-05-20

The national drought situation this week shows that the majority of India remains in the Normal category, with 84.7% of the area classified as Normal. The total area experiencing drought conditions, specifically D0 and above, was 15.3% this week. This figure reflects a contraction in the drought area compared to previous periods, as the total drought area decreased by 0.9 percentage points week-on-week, moving from 16.2% in the last week to 15.3% currently. The regions most affected by drought are NCT of Delhi with 52.2% of its area in drought, followed by Manipur at 42.3%, Mizoram at 40.8%, Arunanchal Pradesh at 40.3%, Jammu & Kashmir at 38.9%, and Meghalaya at 34.1%. Conversely, West Bengal reported 0.0% of its area in drought, while Rajasthan was the least affected state with 1.0% of its area in drought.

Regional conditions this week:
- North: Moderate, scattered drought. About 28% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
- Northwest: Largely normal conditions. About 4% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
- Northeast: Moderate, scattered drought. About 27% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- East: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 7% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
- Central: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 15% of the area is in drought (D0–D4). Pockets of severe-or-worse conditions are present.
- West: Moderate, scattered drought. About 22% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
- South: Mostly normal, with localised dryness. About 16% of the area is in drought (D0–D4).
