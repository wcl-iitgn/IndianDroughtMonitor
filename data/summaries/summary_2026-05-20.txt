# India Drought Monitor — National Summary for week ending 2026-05-20

As of May 20, 2026, India's national conditions remain predominantly normal, with 84.7% of the country classified as such, while 15.3% of the total area experiences drought conditions ranging from abnormally dry to severe. The total drought area has contracted by 0.9 percentage points to 15.3% this week, following a decrease from 16.2% last week and 17.2% approximately one month ago. This week-on-week reduction marks a slight improvement in the overall national drought status, though significant pockets of affected land persist across the subcontinent.

The most severely impacted regions include the NCT of Delhi, where 52.2% of the area is under drought conditions, followed by Manipur at 42.3%, Mizoram at 40.8%, Arunanchal Pradesh at 40.3%, and Jammu & Kashmir at 38.9%. Meghalaya also faces substantial challenges with 34.1% of its area affected. In contrast, the southern and western states show notably better conditions, with West Bengal reporting zero affected area, Rajasthan at 1.0%, Gujarat at 2.1%, and Telangana at 3.4%. While the national trend moves toward recovery, the high concentration of drought in northeastern and northern states indicates that localized water stress remains a critical issue requiring continued monitoring.
